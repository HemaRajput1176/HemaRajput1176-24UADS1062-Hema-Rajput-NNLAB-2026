 I Have MBMU leaf dataset contains images collected only in the summer season To understand how my model would behave in other seasons, I simulated seasonal changes using simple image transformations For spring, I increased contrast and sharpness to represent fresh and clear leaves For autumn I reduced contrast and slightly softened the image to imitate yellowing and aging leaves For winter, I applied blur and dim lighting to represent dry and dull leaves, while summer images were kept original as the baseline.
When I tested the model, it performed best on summer images with 75.68% accuracy In autumn, the accuracy dropped slightly to 62.68% because the softer texture reduced some fine details In spring, the accuracy was 48.64%; although sharper veins helped in some cases, the overall change still affected performance The largest drop was seen in winter, where accuracy became 17.19% because blur removed important fine details like veins and edges that are necessary for classification Among all species, Jamun was the most stable, while paper_flower was affected the most.
The reason the model still shows some robustness is due to SimCLR self-supervised pre-training During training SimCLR uses strong augmentations such as cropping, blurring, flipping, and rotation Because of this, the model learns structural features like leaf shape and vein patterns instead of depending only on brightness and color. A normal supervised model trained only on summer images would likely fail more under these seasonal changes.
